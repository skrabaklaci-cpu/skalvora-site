Automation should begin after process diagnosis, not before it. A strong roadmap turns insight into sequence.

## Start with high-friction, high-frequency steps

Choose processes where delays are frequent and impact is measurable. This improves pilot confidence and stakeholder support.

## Prioritize by effort-to-value ratio

Not every opportunity should be built first. Rank opportunities by expected value, required change effort, and implementation risk.

## Define success metrics before build

Set baseline metrics such as cycle time, error rate, and ownership clarity. Without baselines, impact claims are difficult to validate.

## Build governance into phase one

Include access controls, exception handling, and escalation rules from the beginning. Governance delays are expensive when retrofitted.

## Sequence pilots for learning velocity

Use early pilots to validate assumptions and improve rollout decisions. Automation maturity grows through iteration, not one large launch.

Teams that combine audit discipline with phased execution typically see faster and safer adoption.
